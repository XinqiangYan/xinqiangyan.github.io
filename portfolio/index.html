Yan Lab

Dr. Yan is supervising the Electric and Radiofrequency Lab at Vanderbilt University Institute of Imaging Science (VUIIS). His lab has been supported by eight NIH grants and he is severing as the Principal Investigator of NIH R01 and R21 projects.

He invented lots of groundbreaking hardware devices for MRI and MRgFUS, such as the self-decoupled coil (published in Nature Communication, worldwide patent issued), passive reflectional antenna in MRgFUS (US patent), and RF-transparent B0 shimming coil.

His lab is recruiting research assistants, Ph.D. graduate students, and postdocs to develop the software and hardware technology and transfer these novel technologies to clinical imaging.

Yan Lab

Research Focus

Dr. Yan’s research focuses on developing engineering solutions to the technical challenges in magnetic resonate imaging and MR-guided focused ultrasound, aiming to improve transmit field and B0 homogeneity, reduce the heating near implants, speed up the acquisition speed, reduce the motion artifacts and boost the imaging quality.
